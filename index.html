Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The free encyclopedia that anyone can edit

Wikipedia is a free online encyclopedia with the aim to allow anyone to edit articles. Wikipedia is the largest and most popular general reference work on the Internet and is ranked among the ten most popular websites.

Wikipedia is owned by the nonprofit Wikimedia Foundation.

Here a list of the most important milestones in Wikipedia's birth and growth:

  • March 9, 2000: Nupedia, a free online encyclopedia, was founded
  • January 15, 2001: Wikipedia was launched by Jimmy Wales and Larry Sanger as a side project of Nupedia
  • August 8, 2001: Wikipedia had over 8,000 articles
  • September 25, 2001: Wikipedia had over 13,000 articles
  • June 20, 2003: Wikimedia Foundation was founded with the purpose mantain and develop Wikipedia and other educational content. Another main objective of the Wikimedia Foundation is political advocacy.
  • December 2005: Nature published a peer review comparing 42 science articles from Encyclopædia Britannica and Wikipedia, and found that Wikipedia's level of accuracy approached Encyclopædia Britannica's
  • January 2007: Wikipedia entered for the first time the top-ten list of the most popular websites in the U.S., with 42.9 million unique visitors
  • September 9, 2007: Wikipedia passed the mark of two milion articles
  • August 2009: Wikipedia reached the three milion articles, but the growth of the encyclopedia, in terms of the numbers of articles and of contributors, slowed down.
  • August 8, 2011: during an interview, Wales acknowledged the presence of a slight decline, noting a decrease from "a little more than 36,000 writers" in June 2010 to 35,800 in June 2011
  • 2015: Wikipedia was awarded both the annual Erasmus Prize, which recognizes exceptional contributions to culture, society or social sciences, and the Spanish Princess of Asturias Award on International Cooperation.
  • December 2016: Wikipedia was ranked fifth in the most popular websites globally.
